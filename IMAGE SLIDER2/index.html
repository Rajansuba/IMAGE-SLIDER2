The Japanese Village in Knightsbridge, London, was a late Victorian era exhibition of Japanese culture and located in Humphreys' Hall, which took place from January 1885 until June 1887. Japanese art and culture had become extremely popular in Victorian England by the 1880s, and more than a million people visited the Village. The exhibition employed around 100 Japanese men and women in a setting built to resemble a traditional Japanese village. The exhibit burnt down in May 1885 but was rebuilt and expanded. It reopened the following December.
